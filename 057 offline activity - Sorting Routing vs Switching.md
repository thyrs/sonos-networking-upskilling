## Sorting: Routing vs Switching

### 🟦 Switch (Layer 2 – Local Network Brain)

Drag the concepts that belong to a **SWITCH**:

- **MAC address**  
- **Frame forwarding**  
- **Flooding** (unknown MAC = send everywhere)  
- **Broadcast propagation** (ARP, etc.)  
- **Same network (LAN)**  
- **CAM / MAC address table**  
- **Layer 2** of the OSI model  
- **Fast, simple forwarding**  
- **Does NOT understand IP addresses**  
- **Connects devices inside a network**

---

### 🟥 Router (Layer 3 – Network Boundary Guard)

Drag the concepts that belong to a **ROUTER**:

- **IP address**  
- **Routing table**  
- **Stops broadcasts**  
- **Connects different networks**  
- **Default gateway**  
- **Layer 3** of the OSI model  
- **Path selection** (best route)  
- **Inter-network communication**  
- **NAT** (often, but not always)  
- **Decides where traffic goes next**
